About the C/ALHIV Project

This initiative focuses on breaking stigma, providing psychosocial support, and ensuring children living with HIV have access to health services, education, and safe spaces.
Our advocacy emphasizes community involvement and family-centered care.

What We Do!!

    Counseling:Individual and group counseling sessions for children and families, offering emotional support and guidance for living positively.

    Outreaches:Community and school-based outreach programs to raise awareness, reduce stigma, and connect families to services.

    Health Camps:Organizing health camps with HIV testing, education, and referrals to ensure children receive the care they need.

    Community Dialogues:Engaging parents, leaders, and stakeholders in open discussions to strengthen support systems for children living with HIV.

    Peer Support: Creating safe spaces where children and youth can share experiences, build confidence, and support one another.


Our Impact

Through these activities, we have reached hundreds of children and families, reduced stigma in communities, and built stronger support networks.
Every step brings us closer to a future where children living with HIV thrive with dignity and hope.
